Research

Supersymmetric Gauge Theories & Toric Geometry

My research centers on supersymmetric gauge theories that arise within string theory, with particular emphasis on theories associated to toric Calabi–Yau manifolds. These geometries provide a rich dictionary between gauge theory data and combinatorial structures — lattice polytopes, triangulations, and brane configurations — that encode the physics of the corresponding quantum field theories.

I am a graduate student in the Department of Mathematical Sciences at UNIST, working under the supervision of Professor Rak-Kyeong Seong (personal homepage ↗), whose research group sits at the interface of mathematical physics, quantum field theory, and machine learning.
